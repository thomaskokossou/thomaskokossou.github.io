Job Market Paper
  • Kokossou, T. "High-Speed Mobile Internet, Firms, and Labour Market Outcomes" (Job Market Paper)

    • Abstract: Do businesses benefit from high-speed mobile internet? To deal with the endogeneity of internet infrastructure placement, I simulate how 4G would have been deployed among Ecuador's counties if the deployment of this mobile network infrastructure had been based solely on cost considerations linked to geography. Using this hypothetical rollout as an instrument and data on the universe of formal firms in Ecuador, I estimate how high-speed mobile internet affects employment and wages in firms. Results show an increase in employment and wage per worker, driven respectively by micro firms in low and high internet intensity sectors. Further decomposition by workers’ characteristics shows employment increasing for younger workers and wage for men. Firms’ initial access to internet might explain these findings.
