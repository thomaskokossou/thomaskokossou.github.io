I am an economics PhD candidate at McGill University. My research interests are in Canadian labour markets.
My specialized fields of training are in labour economics, macroeconomic theory, and structural econometrics.

Current work

  • The effect of mismatched demand and supply of labour on recruitment and unemployment in Quebec.
  • The returns to apprenticeship for skilled trades workers.
About me:
I am a Canadian from the west coast in British Columbia. I am a first generation university student and grew up in the lower mainland. After highschool, I attended the University of Victoria and obtained a bachelor of science with honours and then a master's degree in economics. Then I worked for close to a year as an economist with Environment Canada.

I now live and work in Montreal, Quebec. I love hiking, reading, learning French, and more recently I got into running. I casually shoot film photography and paint.
